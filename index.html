Puerto Rican Mofongo

Coral del Mar Murphy will explain how to make, some would say, the most delicious meal in the universe.

Mofongo is fried, smashed plantains in a nutshell (not a literal one). It's probably Puerto Rico's most traditional dish and it is best served with a DIY sauce made of mayo and ketchup, which we call mayoketchup. This dish proves our love for all things fried and made out of plantains!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups vegetable oil
  • 3 plantains
  • 1 tablespoon garlic paste
  • 6 ounces pork rinds or cracklings

¡¡¡IMPORTANTE!!!

"If you don't make mofongo with a pilón, you're not doing it right." -Ancient abuelita (grandma) proverb

I always keep my pilón handy. Also known as a wooden mortar.

Steps

  1. Heat 2 inches of oil in a frying pan or deep fryer to 350 F.
  2. While the oil is heating up, peel the plantains and cut into 1-inch rounds.
  3. Fry the plantains until golden and tender. This will take approximately 4 to 6 minutes.
  4. Remove cooked plantains from the pan or fryer and allow to drain on paper towels.
  5. Put the garlic paste in a mixing bowl or mortar and add the fried plantains. Mash until thoroughly blended.
  6. Add the pork rinds. Continue to mash and mix until all of the ingredients are well incorporated.
  7. Make the mofongo into a half-dome shape using the pilón I am holding. Push a portion of mofongo down to the bottom of the pilon with the pestle.
  8. With the back of a spoon, smooth over and level off the mix.
  9. Then use the spoon to scrape around the bowl and remove the mash in a half-dome shape.
  10. Serve the mofongo hot and add mayoketchup!
